Scatter plots are used to plot two continuous variables on the x and y axes. Click the icon to the left of the axis dropdowns to view the mean and standard deviation for the corresponding values. Under the y-axis dropdown. there is a dropdown labeled with "Color indicates: ", which is used to color the data points by the value they have at a discrete field (like genome). By default the capsids with the discrete field missing are colored black and still plotted; to exclude them select all the checkboxes in that field's dropdown filters. Each point's x, y, and id values can be viewed by mousing over it on the graph.
Bar graphs are used to plot either the count, or mean of a continuous variable, against a discrete variable. Capsids without the selected continuous variable (if it's not count) and the selected discrete variable are excluded from the graph. Mousing over the bars lets you view the x and y coordinate of the bar.
Pie graphs are used to plot the count of each value for a discrete variable. Mousing over the pie slices lets you view the count of capsids with a specific value for the discrete variable. Capsids without the discrete variable are excluded. Mousing over the bars lets you view the x and y coordinate of the bar.
Heatmaps are used to plot either the count, or mean of a continuous variable, for each pair of values for two discrete variables. Mousing over the squares lets you view the count, or mean of the continuous variable, of capsids with the matching discrete values. For example, (16, dsDNA): 3497 is a data point on the graph of average weight by genome by tnumber; the average weight of capsids with tnumber 16 and dsDNA genome is 3497 Da. Capsids that are missing any of the fields are excluded. discrete variable are excluded. Mousing over the bars lets you view the x and y coordinate of the bar.
